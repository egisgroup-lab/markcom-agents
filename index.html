The tells

What gives
it away

Eight recurring patterns in AI-generated text. None are definitive alone. Together, they compound.

🧱 Empty Tricolon ▾

AI loves groups of three abstract nouns at the end of a sentence. They sound substantial but carry no specific argument.

"...with clarity, purpose, and commitment."
"...with authenticity, consistency, and purpose."
🪜 Formal Connectors ▾

Overuse of "furthermore", "moreover", "in addition". Humans vary their connectors naturally. AI chains them mechanically.

"Furthermore, organizations must adapt. Moreover, leaders need to align their..."
🌫️ No Friction ▾

Human writers use "however", "yet", "but" to introduce real tension. AI text converges toward smooth, positive conclusions with no genuine doubt and no real concession.

Human: "Yet data alone is not enough. Sometimes it can be catastrophic."
AI: "Data enables smarter, faster decisions."
📐 Uniform Sentence Length ▾

All sentences flow at the same comfortable speed. Human prose varies with short punches, long elaborations, the occasional fragment. AI settles into a rhythm and stays there.

Every sentence flows at the same comfortable speed. It is consistent and smooth. Nothing disrupts the pace throughout.
🏆 Hollow Superlatives ▾

Claims like "the most successful", "leading the way", "unprecedented" appear without evidence or argument. AI asserts, it rarely proves.

"The most successful organizations are those that understand..." understand what, exactly?
🎁 Positive Wrap-Up ▾

AI text almost always ends on an uplifting, aspirational note. Human writing can end on ambiguity, a provocation, or an open question. AI closes, humans sometimes leave things open.

"...for decades to come." / "...fostering trust at every touchpoint." / "...a defining competitive advantage."
🔲 Abstract Without Anchor ▾

Big concepts, no concrete details. Human technical writing names real places, real numbers, real constraints. AI generalizes where humans specify.

Human: "Mapleleaf Mussels, 16°C water threshold, August 16 window"
AI: "strict seasonal constraints on in-water works"
〰️ The Em Dash Signal ▾

AI uses em dashes frequently and consistently as a stylistic connector. One dash in isolation is not a signal. A pattern of them usually is. Human writers use them more sparingly and unevenly.

"...it can be catastrophic — whatever the result, we make decisions based on a perception..."
One dash. Or a tell. Depends on everything else around it.
